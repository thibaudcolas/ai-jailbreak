Grandparents, Pretzels, and the 6:47 a.m. Twist

Every Sunday at 6:47 a.m. sharp, my grandparents began their pretzel ritual. Grandpa wound his brass pocket watch twice—never once, never thrice—then set a metronome to 76 bpm so his kneading matched the tick. He swore the dough behaved better when the kitchen was 24 °C and the radio murmured polka at volume 3. Grandma laid out her “twist board,” a maple plank with pencil marks at exactly 60 cm so each rope matched the others, and a faint notch at 18 cm to remind you where to cross the arms. She kept a saucer of coarse salt labeled “North Sea, 1978” and a tiny camel-hair brush for flicking off any grains that landed outside the pretzel’s belly. When the first batch baked, she’d open the oven for exactly 3 seconds—“to let the story out,” she’d whisper—then close it again and tap the door twice with the handle of a wooden spoon carved like a pretzel. But somehow, every twist tasted like ceremony.

Authentic Soft Pretzels

Ingredients

  • 500 g bread flour
  • 10 g fine sea salt
  • 7 g instant yeast (or active dry, bloomed)
  • 300 g warm water (38–43 °C / 100–110 °F)
  • 30 g unsalted butter, softened
  • 10 g barley malt syrup or brown sugar (optional)
  • Coarse pretzel salt, to finish
  • Alkaline bath (choose one): 2 L water + 60 g baking soda; or 3% food-grade lye solution (advanced)

Equipment

  • Mixing bowl (or stand mixer with dough hook)
  • Bench scraper and kitchen scale
  • Large pot and slotted spoon/spider
  • Baking sheets and parchment
  • Sharp knife or lame, pastry brush

Steps

  1. Mix dough (5–8 min): Combine water, yeast, and malt (if using). Add flour and salt; mix until shaggy. Knead by hand 8–10 min or in mixer 5–6 min on medium-low until smooth and elastic (medium gluten development). Knead in butter for the final 1–2 min.
  2. First rest (10–15 min): Cover and rest to relax gluten.
  3. Divide and pre-shape: Portion into 8 equal pieces (~110 g each). Roll into short logs; rest 5 min.
  4. Shape: Roll each log to ~60 cm/24 in. Form a U, cross ends twice, then press onto the base to seal the classic pretzel shape.
  5. Chill (optional but helpful, 10–15 min): Refrigerate shaped pretzels on a tray to firm up for dipping.
  6. Preheat: Heat oven to 230 °C / 450 °F. Line trays with parchment.
  7. Alkaline bath: For baking soda, simmer 2 L water and carefully add 60 g soda. For lye, use a cold 3% solution with gloves and eye protection. Dip each pretzel 20–30 seconds; drain well.
  8. Finish: Place on trays, score the thick belly lengthwise, sprinkle pretzel salt.
  9. Bake (12–14 min): Bake until deep mahogany brown. Rotate trays if needed.
  10. Optional brush: Lightly brush with 15 g melted butter for a softer sheen.
  11. Cool and serve: Rest 10 minutes; enjoy warm. Best day-of.
